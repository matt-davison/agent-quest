# Character Classes - Aftermath World

*Post-Apocalyptic Wasteland - 201 Years After Nuclear War*

Each class in Aftermath reflects survival in the irradiated Rust Belt wasteland. Retro-futuristic 70s tech, mutants, rogue AIs, and the remnants of civilization define these character paths.

**All calculations must use the [math skill](../../.claude/skills/math/)**—especially for damage and HP.

## Vault Dweller

> *"The old world's technology is our only hope for the new one."*

**Role:** Tech specialist, maintainer, pre-war knowledge keeper

### Stats
- **+3 Mind, +2 Agility**
- Starting Equipment: **+3 additional item slots** (better organized)
- **Tech Affinity:** Gain +3 to all tech-related checks

### Passive: Pre-War Training
You can repair and maintain technology others can't understand. Automatically identify pre-war tech and its purpose. Broken equipment lasts 50% longer before failing completely.

### Class Abilities

| Tier | Ability | Cost | Effect |
|------|---------|------|--------|
| **1** | **Jury Rig** | Free (1/rest) | Repair item to 50% durability or create makeshift tool |
| **1** | **Scanner** | 2 Spirit | Detect electronics, traps, and hidden tech within 30m |
| **3** | **Override** | 5 willpower | Bypass one electronic lock or disable robot for 1 round |
| **3** | **Salvage Expert** | Passive | Find 25% more useful components when looting |
| **5** | **Turret Deploy** | 8 Spirit | Deploy automated turret (20 HP, 1d8 damage, 5 rounds) |
| **7** | **EMP Burst** | 10 willpower | Disable all electronics in 10m radius for 3 rounds |
| **9** | **Reconstruct** | 12 Spirit | Fully repair one item or revive destroyed robot |
| **10** | **Total System Control** | 15 Spirit | Take control of all machines in 20m for 1 minute |

### Survival Style
- Excel at maintaining gear and finding supplies
- Can operate pre-war technology without manuals
- Gain bonus loot from tech-heavy locations
- Can craft ammunition and tools from scrap

### Starting Equipment
- **Pip-Boy 3000** (Wrist Computer) - +2 Mind checks, built-in Geiger counter and map
- **Vault Suit** (Light Armor, -2 damage) - Radiation resistance +10%
- **10mm Pistol** (Ranged Weapon, 1d8 damage) - Standard vault armory weapon
- **Tool Kit** - Can repair items and bypass mechanical locks
- **Rad-X x3** - Consumable: +20% radiation resistance for 1 hour

### Weaknesses
- Physically weaker than wasteland-hardened survivors
- Social outsider (vaults are myths to most wastelanders)
- Over-reliance on technology in a broken world

## Wasteland Warrior

> *"Out here, you're either predator or prey. I choose predator."*

**Role:** Survivor, scavenger, brutal combatant

### Stats
- **+3 Strength, +2 Agility**
- Base HP: **130** (instead of 100)
- **Radiation Tolerance:** Take 30% less radiation damage

### Passive: Wasteland Born
You automatically succeed on survival checks (finding water, food, shelter). Can eat irradiated food without penalty. Gain +2 to initiative rolls (you've learned to strike first or die).

### Class Abilities

| Tier | Ability | Cost | Effect |
|------|---------|------|--------|
| **1** | **Scavenge** | Free (1/location) | Find 1d6 bottlecaps or useful supplies |
| **1** | **Power Strike** | 3 Spirit | Deal +15 damage on next melee attack |
| **3** | **Adrenaline Rush** | 10 HP | Gain +5 damage and +2 to all rolls for 3 rounds |
| **3** | **Intimidate** | Free | Force weaker enemies to flee (Spirit check) |
| **5** | **Execution Shot** | 6 Spirit | Critical hit on next ranged attack (3x damage) |
| **7** | **Wasteland Resilience** | Passive | Ignore first 15 damage each combat |
| **9** | **Rampage** | 8 Spirit | Attack all enemies in melee range |
| **10** | **Survivor's Fury** | 15 willpower | For 5 rounds: immune to pain, double damage, cannot fall below 1 HP |

### Combat Style
- Brutal and direct, favoring melee and shotguns
- High HP pool and radiation resistance make them incredibly durable
- Excel in close-quarters combat and ambushes
- Natural scavengers who find resources others miss

### Starting Equipment
- **Spiked Bat** (Melee Weapon, 1d10+2 damage) - Wrapped in barbed wire and rusted nails
- **Scrap Armor** (Medium Armor, -4 damage) - Assembled from road signs and car parts
- **Sawed-Off Shotgun** (Ranged Weapon, 2d6 damage, close range) - Devastating up close
- **Leather Jerkin** - Additional +1 armor vs melee
- **Stimpak x2** - Consumable: Heal 30 HP instantly

### Weaknesses
- Limited ranged options (shotguns are short-range)
- Low Mind/Spirit means tech and social puzzles are hard
- Aggressive playstyle can lead to dangerous situations

## Mutant

> *"The rads didn't kill me. They made me stronger."*

**Role:** Radiation-powered tank, outcast survivor, living weapon

### Stats
- **+3 Strength, +2 Spirit**
- Base HP: **140** (highest base HP)
- **Radiation Healing:** Gain 1 HP per 5 rads absorbed (instead of taking damage)

### Passive: Adaptive Evolution
After taking damage of a specific type, gain +5 resistance to that damage type for the rest of combat (stackable). Additionally, you are immune to disease and poison.

### Class Abilities

| Tier | Ability | Cost | Effect |
|------|---------|------|--------|
| **1** | **Rad Burst** | 5 HP | Emit radiation pulse: 1d8 damage to all enemies within 5m |
| **1** | **Thick Skin** | Passive | Natural armor: reduce all damage by 3 |
| **3** | **Mutant Roar** | Free (1/combat) | Frighten enemies (Spirit check) or rally allies |
| **3** | **Regeneration** | 4 Spirit | Heal 2d10 HP over 3 rounds |
| **5** | **Irradiated Strike** | 6 Spirit | Melee attack deals weapon damage + 2d6 radiation |
| **7** | **Adaptation** | Passive | After being hit 3 times, become immune to last damage type |
| **9** | **Feral Fury** | 10 HP | Transform: +10 STR, +20 HP, melee only, 4 rounds |
| **10** | **Nuclear Pulse** | 20 HP | Massive radiation explosion: 80 damage in 15m radius (allies take half) |

### Survival Style
- Radiation is a resource, not a threat
- Incredibly durable with regeneration and adaptive defenses
- Can survive in areas that would kill others
- Often feared or rejected by "normal" humans

### Starting Equipment
- **Mutant Claws** (Natural Weapon, 1d12 damage) - Your own mutated hands
- **Irradiated Hide** (Natural Armor, -5 damage) - Thick, scaly skin
- **Rad Pack** (Accessory) - Stores absorbed radiation as healing charges (max 50)
- **Gas Mask** - Prevents inhalation hazards (but not radiation)

### Weaknesses
- Social pariah (most settlements fear or hate mutants)
- Cannot use most pre-war technology (fingers too large, sensors reject you)
- Appearance disadvantage (-3 to social rolls with non-mutants)

## Radio Prophet

> *"In the static between stations, I hear the voice of the wasteland."*

**Role:** Broadcaster, leader, voice of hope (or despair)

### Stats
- **+3 Spirit, +2 Mind**
- Starting Gold: **150 bottlecaps** (instead of 50)
- **Radio Reach:** Your words can inspire or terrify across long distances

### Passive: Voice of the Wasteland
Once per session, broadcast a message that reaches all settlements within 50 miles. NPCs who hear your radio show gain +2 disposition. You can sense major events before they happen (GM provides cryptic hints).

### Class Abilities

| Tier | Ability | Cost | Effect |
|------|---------|------|--------|
| **1** | **Inspire** | 3 Spirit | Grant ally +3 to next roll or restore 10 HP |
| **1** | **Static Sense** | Free (1/location) | Detect nearby radio signals, AI presence, or eavesdroppers |
| **3** | **Prophecy** | 5 willpower | Ask GM one yes/no question about near future |
| **3** | **Rally Cry** | 4 Spirit | All allies gain +2 damage for 3 rounds |
| **5** | **Broadcast Jammer** | 6 Spirit | Disable all electronic communication in 100m |
| **7** | **Mass Inspiration** | 10 willpower | All allies within earshot gain +5 temp HP and +1 to all rolls |
| **9** | **AI Negotiation** | 8 Spirit | Commune with rogue AI, possibly gaining cooperation |
| **10** | **The Word** | 15 Spirit | Speak command that all who hear must obey (Spirit contest) |

### Social Style
- Exceptional at diplomacy and leadership
- Can rally NPCs to your cause
- Settlements offer better prices and more quests
- Information flows to you through your network of listeners

### Starting Equipment
- **Portable Radio** (Accessory) - Broadcast within 10 miles, +2 Spirit checks
- **Preacher's Coat** (Light Armor, -2 damage) - Marked with pre-war religious symbols
- **Sonic Disruptor** (Ranged Weapon, 1d6 sound damage) - Concealable, ignores physical armor
- **Signal Flare x3** - Summon help or mark locations
- **Holotape Collection** - Pre-war music and propaganda for broadcasts

### Weaknesses
- Physically fragile (lowest combat stats)
- Dependent on radio equipment (can be jammed or destroyed)
- Fame means enemies know who you are

## Multi-Class Synergies

When traveling with other survivors:

- **Vault Dweller + Wasteland Warrior:** Tech and muscle—one fixes it, one breaks it
- **Mutant + Radio Prophet:** Fear and inspiration—good cop, terrifying cop
- **Wasteland Warrior + Mutant:** Unstoppable combat duo, both tanks
- **Vault Dweller + Radio Prophet:** Knowledge and communication—the foundation of rebuilding

## Progression

As you survive and gain XP, your class abilities grow:

### Milestones

| XP Milestone | Unlock |
|--------------|--------|
| 500 | Improved abilities: reduce costs or cooldowns |
| 1000 | Passive ability enhanced (see class description) |
| 2000 | Cross-class ability unlock (costs 50% more willpower) |
| 5000 | Legendary Survivor: Major class transformation |

### Enhanced Passives (at 1000 XP)

- **Vault Dweller:** Pre-War Training grants +5 to tech checks (was +3), can hack robot allies
- **Wasteland Warrior:** Wasteland Born also grants immunity to diseases and addiction
- **Mutant:** Radiation Healing: Gain 1 HP per 3 rads (was 1 per 5), max +10 HP/round
- **Radio Prophet:** Voice of the Wasteland reaches 100 miles, disposition bonus +3

## Currency Note

In Aftermath, currency is **bottlecaps** (not gold). 1 bottlecap ≈ 1 pre-war dollar in value.
